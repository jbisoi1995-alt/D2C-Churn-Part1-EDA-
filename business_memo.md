Business Memo
Subject: Customer Churn Risk Assessment and Retention Priorities
The exploratory analysis identified several important drivers of customer churn.
Key Findings
1.	Customers with high recency values are significantly more likely to churn.
2.	Customers with low purchase frequency demonstrate elevated churn risk.
3.	Lower monetary-value customers churn at substantially higher rates than high-value customers.
4.	Silver-tier customers exhibit higher churn rates compared to Platinum-tier customers.
5.	Customers acquired through Instagram and Google Search channels show relatively higher churn rates than customers acquired through Organic and Referral channels.
6.	Customers with lower engagement metrics, including sessions, product views, and campaign interactions, are more likely to churn.
Recommended Business Actions
1.	Launch re-engagement campaigns targeting customers with high recency.
2.	Create loyalty upgrade programs for Silver-tier customers.
3.	Increase personalized offers for low-frequency purchasers.
4.	Improve onboarding quality for customers acquired through high-risk acquisition channels.
5.	Develop proactive retention programs for customers showing declining website engagement.
Priority Investigation Areas
●	Causes of declining customer engagement.
●	Effectiveness of previous retention campaigns.
●	Customer experience issues leading to inactivity.
●	Relationship between support interactions and churn.
These actions should be investigated before launching large-scale retention initiatives.
